Thesis Offers

Open MSc and BSc thesis topics in sustainable software engineering and Green AI.

Below are the thesis topics I am currently offering at Wageningen University & Research. All topics are connected to my research in sustainable software engineering and Green AI. If one of them draws your attention, read the full description and reach out.

Finding and Fixing Energy Bugs in Software Systems
MSc

Profile, identify, and fix hidden energy inefficiencies in real-world software systems.

Carbon Accounting for Drone Image Datasets and AI Models
MSc

Design a framework to estimate and document emissions of drone imagery datasets and AI models.

Energy Measurement of LLM-Based Agentic Systems
MSc

Design and validate a methodology to measure and analyze energy use of autonomous LLM agent workflows.

Exploring Reproducibility in AI Energy Research
MSc

Explore why AI energy results vary across setups and build practical guidelines for more reliable reporting.

Assessing the Impact of LLM Output Variability on Energy Consumption
MSc

Explore how output-length and response variability in LLMs influence inference energy use and sustainability trade-offs.

How AI Developers Discuss Sustainability Concerns in Practice
MSc

Explore how AI developers talk about energy, carbon, and sustainability trade-offs, and what this means for better tools and practices.

Small Language Models vs Large Language Models: Energy Benefits and Trade-offs
MSc

Compare SLMs and LLMs across tasks to quantify when smaller models provide meaningful energy savings without unacceptable quality loss.
