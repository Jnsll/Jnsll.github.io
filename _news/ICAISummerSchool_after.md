Yesterday I had the pleasure of giving a hands-on workshop at the
**[ICAI Summer School 2026](https://icai.ai/event/icai-summer-school-2026/)**
on measuring and reducing the energy footprint of AI systems.

The session combined a short lecture on energy profiling tools and methods with
a **hackathon-style exercise**: participants measured real-time energy
consumption on a live AI project, and explored the trade-offs between model
accuracy, system performance, and resource usage — all in the name of writing
greener code. The energy and curiosity in the room were remarkable.

Here is how ICAI described the afternoon:

> *"The afternoon featured a highly engaging, interactive workshop led by
> June Sallou from Wageningen University & Research. Attendees participated
> in a hackathon-style energy profiling session. Instead of just talking about
> sustainability, participants measured real-time consumption on an AI project
> playground, navigating the delicate trade-offs between system performance,
> model accuracy, and resource usage to write truly 'greener code'."*
>
> — ICAI Summer School 2026, official communications

Thank you to the organisers and to all participants for making it such a
lively and thought-provoking session. The materials from the workshop will
be shared here shortly.
